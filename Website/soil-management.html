Best Soil Management Practices

Soil Testing

Regular soil testing helps determine pH levels and nutrient content, guiding your fertilization and amendment strategies.

Crop Rotation

Alternate different types of crops in the same area to maintain soil fertility, reduce pest problems, and prevent soil-borne diseases.

Cover Crops

Plant cover crops during off-seasons to prevent soil erosion, suppress weeds, and add organic matter when plowed under.

Organic Matter Addition

Incorporate compost, manure, or green manures to improve soil structure, increase microbial activity, and enhance nutrient content.

Reduced Tillage

Minimize soil disturbance to preserve soil structure, prevent erosion, and maintain beneficial soil organisms.

Proper Irrigation

Implement efficient irrigation systems that provide adequate water without causing erosion or nutrient leaching.
